Hypersurreal

Abstract

The hypersurreal construction focuses on a "tiny time" moment by moment construction starting on Day 0, compared to the surreal construction method which goes day by day starting with Day 1. It proceeds further to explore the class of Oneiric numbers.

Tiny Time

$$+_G = tiny(G) = \{0|\{0|-G\}\}$$ $$-_G = miny(G) = \{\{G|0\}|0\}$$ $$0 = \{-_0|+_0\} =\{\downarrow|\uparrow\}$$ $$* = \{+_0|-_0\} =\{\uparrow|\downarrow\}$$

$\mathbb{O}ne$(iric #s)

Also note that $\frac{1}{\uparrow}$ is not defined since it would be bigger than all surreal numbers and there are no such numbers. (In fact, it does exist but is one of the Oneiric numbers.) - Conway

The gamut of games exhibit symmetry:

$$\omega^{-n} \ll \infty^{-n} \ll n \ll \infty^n \ll \omega^n$$

We also know that there is a gap at the end of the surreal number line - $On$ & that $\frac{1}{On}$ is the gap at the other end:

$$\frac{1}{On} << \omega^{-n} \ll n \ll \omega^n << On$$ $\frac{1}{On} \equiv over$ and $\mathbf{over}>+_{\mathbf{on}}$ $$+_n \ll \uparrow^n \ll \frac{1}{On} << \omega^{-n} \ll n \ll \omega^n << On$$

But we are missing our symmetry..

$$+_n \ll \uparrow^n \ll \frac{1}{On} << n \ll On \ll \frac{1}{\uparrow^n} \ll \frac{1}{+_{n}}$$

This seems to verify what Conway has said ($On < \frac{1}{\uparrow}$).

We know that $+_x<<+_y$ for x > y & that $inf_n(+_n)=+_{On}= tiny$ so we can infer $sup_n(\frac{1}{+_n})=\frac{1}{+_{On}}$ (which we might refer to as $huge$).

$$\frac{1}{tiny}=huge$$

This seems to imply $tiny$ is the infimum & $huge$ the supremum of $\mathbb{O}ne$ - which we might represent as:

$$\mathbb{O}ne=\{huge|tiny\}$$

Additionally, with this symmetry, we could also expect to find things like $\frac{1}{*}$. Say $$\frac{1}{*}=\{I|-I\}=\bigstar_0=giant$$ where $I$ is the oneiric number 1 (eg. $\frac{1}{\uparrow}=\frac{1}{+_0}$).

We would also have $\frac{1}{\circledcirc}$ (where $\circledcirc = \{0*,1*,2*,..\} = sunny$):

$$\frac{1}{\circledcirc}=\{huge|-huge\}=\bigstar_{On}=supergiant$$
